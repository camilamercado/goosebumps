Wendy is right. You have to leave the storehouse. "Okay," you tell her. "I'll go."

A loud neigh sounds from the other side of the storehouse. Wait a minute! This is where your uncle keeps his favorite horse, old Oatbucket! That gives you an idea.

"Come on!" You grab Wendy's hand and pull her to Oatbucket's stall. The frightened horse shakes his brown head at you. His large eyes are wide with fright.

"Easy, boy" You try to sound soothing. You slip a bridle on him and boost Wendy onto his broad back. You know you'll have a much better chance of outrunning the storm on Oatbucket! You unlock the outside door, and the wind yanks it off its hinges! The heavy door rises up into the sky.

Oatbucket neighs loudly and stamps his feet. You know he's scared. But then, so are you!

You swing yourself onto the horse in front of Wendy.

"Come on, boy!" you cry. "Hee-yaa!"

Oatbucket bolts out of the stall, straight into the whirling, blinding dust.
