For the first time you get a good look at the people you've saved. The man is dressed in a plaid flannel shirt and blue jeans. He wears wire-rimmed glasses and has a pleasant, round face.

The woman has long, dark hair pulled back in a tight braid. She's wearing a blue workshirt, jeans, and heavy boots.

"I'm Spin Houston, and this is Sally Turner," the man says: "Thanks again for rescuing us."

"What were you doing out there?" you wonder.

"We're scientists," Sally tells you. "We study tornadoes so we can predict them and save lives."

Too bad they couldn't save your aunt and uncle, you think. You're so sad that you blurt out the story of how your family and farm were destroyed.

When you're done, Spin stares at Sally. Slowly she nods.

"Listen, there's a chance that your aunt and uncle are still alive!" she tells you.

Your mind reels. Whoa! You have to hear more!
