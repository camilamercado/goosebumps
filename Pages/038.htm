Oatbucket gallops faster than you've ever seen him gallop before. You glance behind you. The dark funnel of the tornado is now just a smudge in the distance. You're safe!

Oatbucket comes to a stop. The two of you slide off his back and collapse on the ground.

"We can't hang out in one place for long," Wendy gasps. She peeks over her shoulder.

Oh, no, you think. She can't still believe the twister is following her, can she?

Wendy turns and points back in the direction you came. You take a look and gasp.

The twister - it's moving again! And it's coming straight for you!

"No way!" you shout, getting to your feet. The storm can't actually be following Wendy, can it?

"It's this doll," she moans. She pulls a small doll out of her jacket pocket. It's made of corn husks and beaded cloth.

"A doll?" you cry. "How can the twister be chasing you because of a doll?"

"If you give me a chance, I'll tell you," Wendy says.
