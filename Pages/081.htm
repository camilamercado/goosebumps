The twister flies over the mountain range. And starts panting - gasping for breath.

"What's happening to me?" it cries. Its voice sounds raspy and broken. You notice the air is getting clearer every second.

Yes! you think. Your plan is working! The storm is dying.

"I feel weak," the twister complains. "What have you done?"

"It's the mountains!" you shout. "A twister can only survive on flat land. You're breaking up!"

"You tricked me!" The tornado's voice grows wheezy, then faint. Its blue face flickers. The wind begins to die down. It isn't strong enough to hold you up any longer! As you fall, you turn on your jet pack and float gently to the ground. Yes! The black funnel has collapsed.

Everything caught up in the wind - trees, bits of wood, and dust - starts falling to the earth.

"How could this happen?" the twister gasps.

"Let's face it, twister," you say. "You just ran out of breath."

THE END
