"I found it yesterday in a cave," she explains. "It's a doll of an old Native American wind spirit. I was on a tour with my school. The tour guide told us the old legend. He said the tribe believed that the spirit of the wind is kept in the cave by the doll. But if the doll is removed from its place in the cave, the spirit sends a twister to get it back." She sighs. "I know it was stupid, but I couldn't help myself. The doll was so pretty, I felt like I just had to take it."

You take a deep breath. This story is incredible.

"I'm still not sure I believe you," you tell Wendy. "But if you're telling the truth, why don't you just throw the doll away?"

"Because then the wind spirit will be even angrier," Wendy explains. "And the twister will never stop. But if I can get the doll back to its place in the cave, the tornado will disappear. And the damage it's caused will be undone."
