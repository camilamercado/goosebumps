Of course you'll go into the storm yourself. It may be the only way you'll see your aunt and uncle alive again!

You clear your throat to keep your voice from cracking. You turn to Sally and declare, "I'll do it!"

An hour later you're standing in the middle of a field. Sally bolts the helmet of the storm suit onto your head.

You glance over her shoulder. The huge black twister is racing across the countryside, aimed right at you!

"Good luck," Sally calls. She hops in the van with Spin. The two of them drive off. Soon they're nothing but a black dot far in the distance.

Sally's voice sounds over the transmitter in your ear. "Just relax and let the suit do the work," she urges.

Yeah, sure. Relax, you think. No problem.

Every muscle in your body is tense. The tornado's winds whip violently around you.

"Five, four, three, two, one." Sally's voice counts down. "Release!"
