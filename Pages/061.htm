"You want me to go inside the suit?" you shriek.

Before Sally can answer, the van pulls up a long driveway. It stops in front of a low gray building.

You enter the building. There's so much equipment inside - so many computers! Everything's blipping and bleeping. Cool! For a second you forget your nervousness.

The scientists scurry around, grabbing cables and electronic parts.

Then you see it standing against the wall. The storm suit. You walk closer and touch the shiny chrome sides. You imagine what it would be like inside it, floating into a deadly, raging storm.

"You don't have to," Sally says from behind you.

"I can squeeze into the suit and you can help out on the ground," she offers. "It's up to you."

Your mind races as you consider the choices. With two scientists at the controls and you in the suit, there might be a better chance of finding your aunt and uncle. But you're only a kid. Maybe you should stay on the ground.
