"You have polluted this sacred place!" the spirit booms. As he speaks, the wind in the cavern stirs. "How dare you? What have you to say for yourselves?"

You're too shaky to speak. Besides, you don't know anything about this sacred place or the wind spirit. Wendy's the expert. You nudge her. "Uh-uh-uh," she stutters.

"Answer!" the spirit demands.

But Wendy's teeth are chattering in fear. You realize it's up to you. You just wish you knew what to say.

Hey! Maybe you should just admit that. Aunt Emily always told you, when you didn't know something, you should ask about it.

What if you tell the spirit you don't know anything about Native American culture - and then say you'd like to learn about it? Maybe he'd like that.

Wait, you think. That sounds kind of lame. So lame, the spirit might not believe you. Maybe you should just say you're sorry.
